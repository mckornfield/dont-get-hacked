Summary

Beware the Attackers!
  • 👩‍💻 Hackers → Create malicious software (malware) to compromise machines
  • 🎣 Phishers → Send communications to get information from people
  • 🪪 Social Engineers → Pretend to be someone who has authority to access information
Avoid Where They Lurk!
  • 📵💬 Ignore emails, phone calls, and texts from unknown sources
  • 🔞🔓 Stay away from suspicious websites, unsecured websites and public Wi-Fi
  • 💻👀 Don’t leave your hardware unattended
  • 🚫🔌 Don’t plug strange devices/USBs into your computer
  • 🚫💿 Don’t install free software
Know Your Defenses!
  • 👯‍♀️ Find your tech buddy and use them to help you out!
  • 🛡️ Browse the web with a good ad-blocker
  • 🛂 Use a password manager to manage and set your passwords
  • 🔑 Use Multifactor Authentication (MFA) wherever possible
  • 1️⃣ Use a Strong Single Sign On if MFA is unavailable
  • Backup your 📸 Photos,📑 Documents, 🖥️ Computer and 📱Phone to the cloud and an external drive
  • 🎥 Secure your video calls (use passwords, share privately, disable screen sharing)
  • 🆕 Keep your operating systems (smartphone/computer) up-to-date!
  • 💬 If you can’t be trusted to safely use technology, ask your tech buddy for help!
Prepare For the Future!
  • 🗞️Stay informed on the latest in Artificial Intelligence and Quantum Computing
  • 🗳️Support politicians that will fund essential AI/Quantum Computing research
Matt’s recommended purchases/installs:
  • Gifts for your tech buddy!
  • A yearly subscription to a team/family Password Manager
  • (I use LastPass, but Keeper and Bitwarden come well recommended)
  • A yearly subscription to a Cloud Storage account
  • I use Google Photos/Docs, but iCloud or OneDrive are good as well
  • A Yubikey!
  • (Free) An authenticator app (Duo Mobile or Google Authenticator)
  • (Free) UBlock Origin extension for your browser
  • Physical screen covers for your laptop/webcam
